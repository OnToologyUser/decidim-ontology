Assemblyc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://ontology.decidim.org/decidim_roadmap/Assembly

An assembly is a participation space, defined as a structured group of participants that meet periodically, individually or representatively, and can create proposals and make decisions. Beyond being a Participation space, an assembly is also a Group, and as such it can be the promoter of a Process, or participate in other participation spaces. An Assembly may have WorkingGroups, i.e. subgroups created to work on specific issues.

Participation functionalityc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://ontology.decidim.org/decidim_roadmap/ParticipationFunctionality

This class includes the different functionalitites that can be added to a ParticipationSpace by RegisteredParticipants having administrative role to configure it. Each ParticipationFunctionality has to be linked to at least one participationSpace it belongs to, through the property functionality_of. Furthermore, a ParticipationFunctionality needs to have a Configurator that allows its configuration by participants having the permissions to do so. Some functionalitites represent one single element that is added to the ParticipationSpace, such as a Page, a Votation or a Survey, while other functionalities are elements that contain participationItems, such as Proposals, BudgetedProposals, Meetings or Comments; such functionalitties that contain ParticipationItems are defined as ParticipationContainers. While some functionalities are general and can belong to any kind of ParticipationSpace, such as a Page, a Blog or a Debate, other are specific to a certain kind of participationSpace. E.g., a WorkingGroup can only belong to an Assembly. In the latter case, the range of the property functionality_of for that particular ParticipationFunctionality is restrictetd to the kind of space it can belong to.
has super-classes
Participation artifact c
functionality of op some Participation space c
has configurator op some Configurator c
has sub-classes
Newsletter c, Page c, Participation container c, Phase c, Search engine c, Survey c, Votation c, Working group c
is in range of
functionality of op, has functionality op

Participation spacec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://ontology.decidim.org/decidim_roadmap/#ParticipationSpace

ParticipationSpaces are the frameworks in which participation takes place. They represent the channels to which the citizens or the members of an organization can articulate collective needs and proposals, and take decisions. There are different kinds of spaces: Processes, Initiatives, Assemblies, Consultations. Additionally, ProcessGroups are spaces that include and group together more than one Process. A ParticipationSpace is vinculated to an Agent that promotes it, usually a SovereignOrganization that aims to involve its members or the citizenry in a collective decision making effort. A ParticipationSpace is specified by a TerritorialScope and a SectorialScope that delimit its scope. A ParticipationSpace has some Configurator that allows its configuration and management by RegisteredParticipants having an administrative role that grants them permissions over the ParticipationSpace.
has super-classes

has configurator op some Configurator c
has promoter op some Agent c
has scope op some Sectorial scope c
has scope op some Territorial scope c
has platform op exactly 1 Participation platform c
has sub-classes
Assembly c, Consultation c, Initiative c, Process c, Process group c
is in domain of
functionality of op, has functionality op, has platform op, has promoter op
is in range of
platform of op, promoter of op
